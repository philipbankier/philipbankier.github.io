07 · The One-Line Takeaway From Each
Models ship like product features now.

6 weeks between GPT-5.4 and 5.5. The labs are racing to lock down agent categories, not publish papers. Plan stack updates monthly, not quarterly.

The moat is in the harness, not the model.

A well-engineered Sonnet deployment beats a badly-engineered Opus one at a fraction of the cost. Context engineering, memory, and skills are what differentiate.

The techniques exist. The product doesn't.

Activation steering, ACE playbooks, and KTO feedback live in three disconnected research communities. Nobody has assembled them for a non-technical user yet.

Files as memory, not chat as memory.

Codex should anchor continuity in ~/agent-ops, not in the context window. Write handoffs to disk before compacting. This is the cultural shift from Claude to Codex.

Trust is the binding constraint.

84% of devs use AI daily, only 29% trust it in production. 28K exposed OpenClaw instances. The gap is verification, not capability.

Substack has a real API.

python-substack is legit: full draft→publish workflow, markdown conversion, image upload, cookie auth. Could replace browser-based publishing entirely.
